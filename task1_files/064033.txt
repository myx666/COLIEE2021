 Lutfy, C.J. 
 :  
 UPON 
  this application for judicial review of the negative decision, dated April 29, 2005, by the Refugee Protection Division that the applicants, who are citizens of Nigeria, are neither Convention Refugees nor persons in need of protection; 
 
 
 
 [2] 
   
 UPON 
  the applicants' attempt to introduce a letter from a Nigerian friend purporting to describe the circumstances of the alleged assassination in 2004 of the brother of the applicant Christopher Ayalogu; 
 
 
 
 [3] 
   
 UPON 
  the acknowledgement of both parties that the letter was produced for the first time by the applicants at the hearing of April 13, 2005 and not twenty days before the hearing as required by Rule 29 of the Refugee Protection Division Rules, SOR/2002-228 ("the Rules"); 
 
 
 
 [4] 
   
 UPON 
  the Court's review of the transcript of the hearing where the member stated: "if the letter has not been produced by now, I'm not going to look at it." (page 674 of the tribunal record); 
 
 
 
 [5] 
   
 UPON 
  the presiding member's failure to consider the letter's "relevance and probative value" as required by Rule 30 (a) of the  <FRAGMENT_SUPPRESSED> ; 
 
 
 
 [6] 
   
 UPON 
  the hearing of March 20, 2006; and 
 
 
 
 [7] 
   
 UPON 
  the respondent's letter, filed on March 22, 2006, acknowledging the presiding member's failure to apply Rule 30; 
 
 
 
 [8] 
   
 IT IS HEREBY ORDERED 
  that: 
 
 
 
 1. This application for judicial review is granted and the matter is referred to a different member of the Refugee Protection Division for rehearing and re-determination.
